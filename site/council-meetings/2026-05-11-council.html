Public Comment – Legislative Agenda
30:42
Public Comment – Anita Russo
Schenectady Tennis Association (STA) president Anita Russo expressed concerns about the function and implementation of the proposed electronic lock system to protect the new tennis courts in Central Park. Russo shared worries that the system may present new barriers to play and suggested a balance between maintaining public, low-cost access while being able to host tournaments that support the local economy.
32:19
Public Comment – John Knudson
Resident John Knudson shared concerns about the proposed fees to use the Central Park tennis courts. He noted that, across the U.S., public tennis courts are generally free, and that only New York City charges for use. Knudson broke down what a $15/hour fee would mean for an active tennis player, explaining that the cost would become exorbitant over a season, especially compared to the previous cost, which was $0. He believes the fee would present a barrier to all tennis players, especially to learners and the youth.
36:34
Public Comment – Jonathan Simms
Resident Jonathan Sims echoed Knudson’s concerns and added that the city should use real grass instead of seed for the project so the area doesn’t end up full of weeds again. He also said a $15/hour tennis fee doesn’t make sense, especially when the courts sit empty during the day and residents already pay taxes for the park.
38:33
Public Comment – Marion Porterfield
Resident and former city councilmember Marion Porterfield shared concerns about the proposed fees to the Central Park tennis courts. Taxpayers already paid for most of the new courts through ARPA funds and city dollars, so residents should not be charged fees to use them. She noted that the pool, built at a much higher cost, is free, that the new courts require little maintenance, and that the USTA will use the courts for 20 years at no cost, which she argued should entitle residents to the same consideration.
Public Comment – General City Business
41:36
Public Comment – John Knudson
Resident John Knudson explained that from 2010 to 2024 the city paid nothing to operate the tennis courts because volunteers handled basic upkeep. He argued that the new courts are built to last 25 years with almost no maintenance, so charging $15/hour would unfairly block people from teaching their kids, practicing, or playing casually.
44:38
Public Comment – Rachael Ward
Resident and city council candidate Rachael Ward thanked city staff and the council for improvements in Central Park. She suggested restoring the old Central Park Flyer ride.
45:43
Public Comment – Sean Radigan
Resident Sean Radigan requested that the City share the budget as a spreadsheet rather than a PDF. He argued that a “smart city” should provide prompt, accurate, and accessible public data.
48:09
Public Comment – Eric Johnson
Resident Eric Johnson thanked the Council for working to bring order to what he sees as an out‑of‑control and costly garbage system. He urged better protection for sanitation workers and reported continued illegal dumping on Wiley Street.
50:26
Public Comment – Elizabeth Walsh
Resident Elizabeth Walsh thanked the Mayor, Council, and staff for their public service and celebrated the collaborative spirit in Schenectady. She highlighted improvements in Central Park and invited the community to upcoming Schenectady Foundation events.
52:42
Public Comment – Orlondo Otis Huntley
Resident and mayoral candidate Orlondo Otis Huntley said he has been visiting local small businesses and hearing about the challenges they face. He urged the City to consider ways to encourage local spending.
