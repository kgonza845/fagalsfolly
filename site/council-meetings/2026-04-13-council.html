Public Comment – General City Business
10:20
Public Comment – Cynthia Farmer
Resident Cynthia Farmer urged the City to address widespread garbage and deteriorating vacant properties across Schenectady. She described specific problem areas on Lottridge Hill, Albany Street, and Swan Street. Farmer stated she has not seen the Mayor come through any neighborhoods since his reelection and urged him to walk the areas to see conditions firsthand. She called on the City to take responsibility for cleanup of vacant parcels and improve how the City looks.
13:28
Public Comment – Vince Riggi (former councilmember)
Former Council Member Vince Riggi questioned multi‑year surpluses in the City’s waste fund and argued it should be segregated like water and sewer. He raised concerns about the proposed tiered trash limits, noting that bags not placed inside the standardized cans would not be collected, which he said would burden, particularly for seniors and residents without backyard access. Riggi urged the Council to review the line‑item budget documents he provided.
17:22
Public Comment – Kelly Horton
Resident Kelly Horton told the Council she was evicted from her home earlier that morning for unpaid taxes. The person she was with said she has been trying to pay the taxes since May of last year and had checks mailed back to her from the tax department. Horton asked the Council to review her situation and said she wants her home back after 29 years of ownership.
18:57
Public Comment – Eric Johnson
Wylie Street Cleanup organizer, Electric City Community Grocery Board President, and Resident Eric Johnson described a two‑day Wiley Street cleanup this past weekend and said illegal dumping over the embankment continues despite prior requests for mitigation. He outlined the city‑owned parcels involved, suggested barriers to deter dumping, and urged Council members to list their phone numbers on the City website since he was not able to invite them all to the cleanup due to that barrier. Johnson also invited the Council to tour the Electric City Community Grocery.
23:01
Public Comment – Dean Mirabito
Resident Dean Marabito urged the Council to consider expanding the Schenectady Community Compost Project, launched in 2013. He highlighted the environmental benefits of composting and noted that the benefits far away the challenges.
24:40
Public Comment – William Rivas
C.O.C.O.A House Executive Director and Resident William Rivas described a decade of community–police trust‑building work he founded through Save Our Streets, including events, dialogues, and partnerships that led to the Schenectady Police Community Hiring Panel. He noted the panel’s national and academic recognition and emphasized that its success comes from shared participation among residents, police leadership, and City officials. Rivas urged the Council to ensure the voices involved remain part of all related conversations as they discuss.
28:45
Public Comment – Marion Porterfield (former councilmember)
Former Councilmember and Resident Marion Porterfield thanked volunteers and One Schenectady for the Wiley Street cleanup. She called for a permanent solution, noting neighbors want fencing and cameras and that last year’s request for a fence was not acted on. Porterfield thanked Councilman Wiggins for convening the Housing Stability Task Force and highlighting the need for permanent housing solutions for people living in hotels.
Miscellaneous Business

Councilmember Kim Wiggins – Housing Stability Task Force Update
Councilmember Wiggins reported that the first Housing Stability Task Force meeting was highly informative and said the group has the right people to make meaningful progress. She outlined expectations for future meetings, stating that side conversations, distractions, or behavior that discourages participation with not be allowed.
